== Verification Method

Verification methods based on this Recommendation are given in <<appendix-a>>. Other
verification methods may be selected depending on the number and quality of the thermometers
to be verified and the resources of the verifying authorities (personnel, equipment, etc.). The
method recommended below can be applied without difficulty for verifying up to 1 million
thermometers a year.

=== Laboratory equipment

==== Laboratory

===== Protection from the dangers of mercury

The floor and tables on which the testing is carried out must be smooth and impervious.
The tables must have raised edges. Adequate floor-level ventilation must be provided.

===== Lighting

Special lighting is required for examining and reading the thermometer.

===== Safety devices for stamping machines

The stamping machines must be equipped with safety devices.

Instruments and auxiliary devices for determining the errors of indication.

[[cls-A.1.2.2.1]]
====== {blank}
Well-stirred water baths with interchangeable thermometer-holders. The
thermometers under examination must be immersed in the bath to the lowest numbered line
on the scale.

During measurement, the temperature of the water baths must be constant to within
stem:[0.02 "unitsml(degC)"] and uniform to within better than stem:[0.01 "unitsml(degC)"].

The use of baths with automatic temperature control is recommended.

There must be enough water to ensure that immersion of the thermometers in the bath
will not cause a temperature drop greater than stem:[0.05 "unitsml(degC)"]. Time can be saved when testing by
preheating the thermometers in a special bath to a few tenths of a degree Celsius below the test
temperature.

[[cls-A.1.2.2.2]]
====== {blank}
Standard thermometers, e.g., total immersion mercury-in-glass thermometers
with scale intervals of stem:[0.02 "unitsml(degC)"] or stem:[0.05 "unitsml(degC)"] and an auxiliary scale for stem:[0 "unitsml(degC)"], or platinum resistance
thermometers.

Preferably two standards thermometers are used to measure the temperature of the baths
(<<cls-A.3.1>>) and a third standard thermometer to check the first two.

The corrections for standard thermometers, including the correction at stem:[0 "unitsml(degC)"] must be
determined to within an uncertainty of measurement of preferably stem:[pm 0.01 "unitsml(degC)"] or a maximum
stem:[pm 0.02 "unitsml(degC)"].

====== {blank}
Magnifying glasses with at least stem:[xx 4] magnification for reading standard enclosedscale
mercury-in-glass thermometers.

Monocular or binocular viewers with approximately stem:[xx 10] magnification for reading
standard solid-stem mercury-in-glass thermometers.

[[cls-A.1.2.2.4]]
====== {blank}
A centrifuge with a container for thermometers to drive down the mercury column in
thermometers under test. The centrifuge must be capable of exerting an acceleration of
stem:[600 "unitsml(m/s^2)"] on the mercury at the level of the base of the thermometer bulbs (see <<cls-5.4>>).

[[cls-A.1.2.2.5]]
====== {blank}
A zero point (stem:[0 "unitsml(degC)"]) ice bath for testing standard thermometers{blank}footnoteblock:[f1]. The device consists
of a Dewar flask filled with finely crushed ice covered over with water. The water used to
make the ice and the water in which the ice is submerged must be pure. Its electrical
conductivity must not exceed stem:[10^-3 "unitsml(S*m^-1)"] at stem:[20 "unitsml(degC)"]. The ice must be carefully tamped so that
there are no air bubbles in the ice-water mixture. It must be compacted as much as possible
both prior to measurement and periodically during measurement.

It is recommended that a water purifier, a refrigerator with ice trays, and an ice crusher be
obtained for preparing the ice-water mixture.

=== Determination of errors of indication

[[cls-A.3.1]]
==== {blank}
The thermometers must be examined for compliance with the provisions of <<cls-8>> with
regard to maximum permissible errors. This examination is performed by comparing the test
thermometers with standard thermometers (<<cls-A.1.2.2.2>>) in well-stirred water baths
(<<cls-A.1.2.2.1>>).

==== {blank}
The thermometers must be checked at a minimum of two temperatures spaced stem:[4 "unitsml(degC)"] apart
(test temperatures) in the stem:[35.5 "unitsml(degC)"] to stem:[42 "unitsml(degC)"] range.

===== {blank}
When the temperature of the water bath is again sufficiently constant (see

the standard thermometers, whereupon the thermometers under examination are removed

When measuring temperature, the corrections to the standard thermometer indications

thermometer errors at the test temperature.

=== Testing the maximum device

==== {blank}
The maximum device is tested for compliance with the requirements of <<cls-5.4>>.

==== {blank}
Immediately after the test at the maximum test temperature, all of the thermometers
under examination are placed, blub outwards, in a centrifuge (<<cls-A.1.2.2.4>>) and their
mercury columns are made to recede.

In accordance with <<cls-5.4>>, the speed of the centrifuge is regulated so that the mercury
at the bottom of the bulbs is subjected to an acceleration of stem:[600 "unitsml(m/s^2)"]. Once this acceleration is
reached, the centrifuge is switched off.

All thermometers in which the mercury column has not dropped below the lowest
numbered line must be rejected.

During this test, the ambient temperature must be lower than the minimum scale value of
the thermometers.

=== Testing the solid-stem thermometer coloration

The scale, numbering and inscriptions will be examined to assure that they have been
engraved or indelibly printed in compliance with <<cls-6;and!cls-7>>.

The thermometers are immersed for one hour in a 5 % aqueous solution of phenol, the
temperature of which must be between stem:[20 "unitsml(degC)"] and stem:[30 "unitsml(degC)"], or are subjected to the action of 96 %
alcohol. The thermometers are then wiped with a. piece of lightcolored cloth which should not
pick up any stains.

=== Testing the materials

Because the examination which pertains to compliance with the provisions of <<cls-4>>
(Materials) cannot be conducted on the thermometers themselves, the manufacturer of clinical
thermometers (mercury-in-glass, with maximum device) must prove to the verifying authorities
that the materials used meet the requirements of <<cls-4>>.

When considering the test methods to be used in case of doubt, the following provisions
should be taken into account:
